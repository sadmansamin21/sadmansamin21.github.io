Evaluation Update: 2026

Scores evaluate institutional agility relative to rapid automation shifts. Ratings assess four core parameters:

1. AI-Adaptation (35%): Integration of computational models into standard programs, use of interactive teaching tools, and general curriculum flexibility.

2. AI-Proof Pathways (35%): Focus on human-centric fields such as manual engineering prototyping, lab chemistry, field biology, and clinical practice.

3. Infrastructural Shifts (20%): Availability of computing clusters, specialized cleanrooms, high-tech labs, and decentralized internal administrative frameworks.

4. Friction Coefficient (10%): Administrative reaction times, political or structural disruptions, and overall institutional flexibility.

Foresight FAQ

Ratings analyze the operational alignment between current degree frameworks and automated workforce demands. Evaluated points focus on whether an institution relies on outdated theoretical syllabi or prioritizes practical, unstructured, and laboratory-intensive research.
Tracks high in manual, formula-based logic (such as traditional accounting bookkeeping, standard software programming layouts, or rote civil structure stress math) are highly exposed. These areas are increasingly handled directly by cognitive model pipelines.
